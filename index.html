Gender: Male

Case #7: View A.

Age: 25 years

Gender: Male

Case Notes: Air space consolidation is present at the right lower lobe posterobasal and superior segments. Ground glass opacity nodule also is seen at the right middle lobe lateral segment.

Diagnosis: The interesting point of this case is that there is a large unilateral area of air space consolidation involving the right lower lobe but the ground glass and/or consolidative opacities of COVID-19 pneumonia are most commonly bilateral. This patient had positive RT-PCR testing for COVID-19.

Case #10: View A.

Age: 50 years

Gender: Female

Case Notes: Ground glass nodule is present at the left lower lobe laterobasal segment.

Diagnosis: When the patient referred to our imaging center for chest CT, we saw only a small ground-glass nodule at the left lower lobe. So regarding the clinical presentations and high prevalence of COVID-19 pneumonia in Iran, we repeat the chest CT, 48 hours later and finally, we found the new ground-glass opacities in the right lower lobe. Subsequently, the patient underwent RT-PCR COVID-19 assay and the result was positive.

Case #12: View A.

Age: 70 years

Gender: Male

Case Notes: There are large areas of ground glass opacities in the lower right lobe, in the upper lobes, with interlobular septal thickening in the subpleural area. Paraseptal emphysema is present in the upper lobes. No evidence of mediastinal adenopathy.

Diagnosis: In the right clinical context large ground-grass opacity lesions, predominantly in the peripheral and posterior lungs on CT, are diagnostic of COVID-19 pneumonia. Note the absence of mediastinal adenopathy which is not usually seen in COVID-19 and should suggest the presence of superadded infection, e.g. bacterial pneumonia.

What is the purpose of this primer?

The goal of this primer is to provide an engaging, easy-to-use interface for understanding the classic radiologic imagery associated with COVID-19. Currently, the online platform features 46 CT stacks and accompanying case information that we hope medical students and other medical trainees can use.

Is this project complete?

No, it isn't! Now that this website is set up, we would like to incorporate X-ray imagery, in addition to more CT stacks. If you are a software engineer, web designer, diagnostic radiologist, or other medical imaging expert, please contact us.
